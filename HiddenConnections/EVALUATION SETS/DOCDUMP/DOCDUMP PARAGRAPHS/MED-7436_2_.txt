Meals high in animal fat impair endothelial function for 4 h, an effect that can be minimized by pretreatment with antioxidant vitamins. Dietary cholesterol (one egg yolk), though it increases fasting low density lipoproteins by only about 10%, increases oxidized low-density lipoproteins by 34%. Two studies in coronary patients, the Lyon Diet Heart Study and the IndoMediterranean Diet study, have shown that, despite no difference in fasting lipid levels, a Mediterranean diet reduced myocardial infarction and death by 60% in four years and by 50% in two years compared with diets amounting to the American Heart Association diet that is usually prescribed to coronary patients.
