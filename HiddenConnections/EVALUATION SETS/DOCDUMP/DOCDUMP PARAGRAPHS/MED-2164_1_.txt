The association of lifestyle and facial ageing has been less well studied. OBJECTIVES: To identify lifestyle factors that associate with perceived facial age in white north European men and women. METHODS: Lifestyle, facial wrinkling and perceived facial age were studied in two cross-sectional studies consisting of 318 Dutch men and 329 women aged 45-75 years who were part of the Leiden Longevity Study, and 162 English women aged 45-75 years who were nonsmokers.
