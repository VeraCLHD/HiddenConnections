Abstract BACKGROUND: Very long chain omega-3 fatty acids (w-3 PUFA) intake and fish consumption have been suggested as protective factors against neuropsychiatric disorders but there is scarcity of large cohort studies assessing this association. AIM OF THE STUDY: To assess the association between w-3-PUFA intake and fish consumption and mental disorders. METHODS: A prospective cohort study was performed in 7,903 participants.
