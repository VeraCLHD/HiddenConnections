Summary As the population ages, there is an ever-increasing need for therapeutic agents that can be used safely and efficaciously to manage symptoms related to postmenopausal estrogen deficiency. Endogenous estrogens, e.g., 17Î²-estradiol, of exogenous mammalian origin, e.g., horses, have long been used to manage such symptoms. There are more than twenty different classes of phytochemicals that have demonstrated affinity for human estrogen receptors in vitro.
