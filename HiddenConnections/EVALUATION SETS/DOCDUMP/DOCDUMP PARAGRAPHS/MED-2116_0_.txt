Abstract BACKGROUND: Di-2-ethylhexylphthalate (DEHP) is an ester of phthalic acid commonly found in processed foods. DEHP may contribute to obesity and insulin resistance in children and adolescents, yet dietary exposures have been not been studied in this vulnerable subpopulation. OBJECTIVE: To assess diet and its relation to urinary phthalates in a nationally representative sample of US children and adolescents.
