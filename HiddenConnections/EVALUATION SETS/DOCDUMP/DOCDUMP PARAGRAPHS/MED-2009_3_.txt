OBJECTIVE: To examine associations between urinary BPA concentration and body mass outcomes in children. DESIGN, SETTING, AND PARTICIPANTS: Cross-sectional analysis of a nationally representative subsample of 2838 participants aged 6 through 19 years randomly selected for measurement of urinary BPA concentration in the 2003-2008 National Health and Nutrition Examination Surveys. MAIN OUTCOME MEASURES: Body mass index (BMI), converted to sex- and age-standardized z scores and used to classify participants as overweight (BMI ≥85th percentile for age/sex) or obese (BMI ≥95th percentile).
