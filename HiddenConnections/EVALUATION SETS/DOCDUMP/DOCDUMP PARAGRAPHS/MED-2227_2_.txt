The publicity given the reports of cancer prevention by antioxidants will probably increase self-supplementations. It is therefore necessary to understand the pharmacological limits of such supplementation with vitamin E. Daily supplementation of adults with about 200 mg of d-alpha-tocopherol equivalents per day has not been proven to be harmful, but the effects of ingestion of more than 800 mg a day have not been studied sufficiently. Special precautions should be taken in administering emulsified vitamin E preparations intravenously to premature infants.
