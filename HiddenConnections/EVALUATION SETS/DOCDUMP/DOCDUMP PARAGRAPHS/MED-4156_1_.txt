The study population consisted of 148 men in 1960 and 42 men in 1991 of the same age group (fifty-five to fifty-nine years old) and from the same rural area. All men had a complete examination of the cardiovascular system and a resting electrocardiogram (ECG). Systolic BP (SBP) > or = 140 mmHg was found in 42.6% of the subjects in 1960 and in 45.2% in 1991 (NS).
