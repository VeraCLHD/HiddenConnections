The "World Protein Gap" was considered the major cause of infant mortality and retarded development in the Third World but a problem that could be solved by the application of sophisticated technology. It then appeared that such technology was in general inapplicable to the real-life situation of more primitive communities. It was also found that a more equitable supply of ordinary foods could supply adequate protein, provided that, in the case of infants, attention was paid to their not being too bulky to allow adequate energy intake.
