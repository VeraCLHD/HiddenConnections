The same staff should care for the control patients as well as those in protected environments. In addition, control patients in some studies have been clearly different in the severity of the underlying disease from patients placed in protected environments. Even without these proper controls, differences in infection rates have varied considerably among the studies both inside and outside the protected environment and efficacy has also varied considerably; however, the one constant in almost every controlled study is that life has not been prolonged, remission induction increased, nor remission duration prolonged.
