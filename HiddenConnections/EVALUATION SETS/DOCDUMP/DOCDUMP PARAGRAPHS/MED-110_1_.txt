Objective To determine whether milk consumption during teenage years influences risk of hip fracture in older adults and to investigate the role of attained height in this association. Design Prospective cohort study over 22 years of follow-up Setting United States Participants Over 96,000 Caucasian postmenopausal women from the Nurses’ Health Study and men age 50 and older from the Health Professionals Follow-up Study Exposures Frequency of consumption of milk and other foods during ages 13–18 and attained height were reported at baseline.
