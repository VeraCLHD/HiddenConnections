results: consumption of a hazelnut-enriched diet significantly improved fmd (56.6%), total cholesterol (-7.8%), triacylglycerol (-7.3%), ldl-cholesterol (-6.17%), and high-density lipoprotein cholesterol (6.07%) compared with the control diet i. oxidized-ldl, hs-crp, and svcam-1 levels were significantly lower in the group ingesting a hazelnut-enriched diet compared with the control diets i and ii.
