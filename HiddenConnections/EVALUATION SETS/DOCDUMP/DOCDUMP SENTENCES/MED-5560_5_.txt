over the last few decades there has been increasing interest in the possible role of curcumin in ibd and several studies with various experimental models of ibd have shown it to be effective in mediating the inhibitory effects by scavenging free radicals, increasing antioxidants, influencing multiple signaling pathways, especially the kinases (mapk, erk), inhibiting myeloperoxidase, cox-1, cox-2, lox, tnf-α, ifn-γ, inos; inhibiting the transcription factor nf-κb.
